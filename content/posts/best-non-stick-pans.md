Non-stick pans are the workhorse of most kitchens: eggs, pancakes, delicate fish, anything that would otherwise fight you on cleanup. But not all non-stick coatings are created equal, and the category has a wide range of quality and price. Here's what to actually look for, and a few picks across different budgets.

## What to Look For

Coating durability matters more than almost anything else. Cheap non-stick pans lose their coating within months of regular use, while better ones can last years with proper care.

A few other things worth checking before buying:

- Handle material and heat resistance (stainless steel handles generally outlast plastic ones, and many are oven-safe)
- Base thickness, since thin pans warp faster and heat unevenly
- Whether it's PFOA-free, which most modern non-stick cookware is, but worth confirming
- Oven-safe temperature rating if you want flexibility beyond stovetop use

## Budget Pick

Look for an aluminum-core pan with a reinforced non-stick coating in the 8 to 10 inch range. These typically run affordably and are a solid starting point if you're replacing a worn-out pan without a big investment.

## Mid-Range Pick

A step up usually means a thicker aluminum or stainless-core base with a multi-layer non-stick coating, often rated for higher heat and more scratch resistance. These tend to hold their coating noticeably longer than budget options.

## Premium Pick

At the higher end, ceramic-reinforced or titanium-reinforced coatings offer the longest lifespan and best heat distribution, often with metal-utensil-safe surfaces (though a silicone or wood utensil is still gentler long-term). These usually come with the longest warranties too.

## Caring for Non-Stick Pans

- Use low to medium heat; non-stick coatings degrade faster at high temperatures
- Avoid metal utensils unless the pan is specifically rated for them
- Hand wash when possible, even if labeled dishwasher-safe, to extend coating life
- Never use cooking spray if avoidable, since it can build up a sticky residue that's hard to remove and can affect performance over time

## Quick FAQ

**How long should a non-stick pan last?**
A well-cared-for mid-range or premium pan can last 3 to 5 years or more. Budget pans often need replacing within a year or two of regular use.

**Is it safe to use a scratched non-stick pan?**
Once the coating is visibly flaking or scratched down to the base metal, it's time to replace it rather than keep cooking with it.

**Ceramic vs traditional non-stick, which is better?**
Ceramic coatings tend to be more heat-resistant and free of certain chemical coatings, but often don't last quite as long as high-quality traditional non-stick before losing their slickness.
